Embodied Agentic STEM Education: Effects of 3D VR Compared to 2D PC

This randomized control trial determined how two levels of embodiment (low versus high) and platform (2D PC versus 3D Virtual Reality) affected learning in an interactive game about Batesian Mimicry.

Platform is not Destiny

Goal of Project

The objective of this study is to determine how levels of embodiment might affect learning on two different platforms.

Project Team

Mina C. Johnson, Vanessa Ly, Man Su, Ricardo Nieland Zavala, Hannah Bartolomea, Elena Kalina

My Role

I worked with my research professor to design 10 item pre, post, and follow up tests. I also was responsible to run over 4 user studies per week and work with the team to synthesize results.

Project Outcome

Data collected indicated a main effect of embodiment and an interaction of embodiment by platform. This showed that active embodiment groups learned more content and that the low embodied VR group learned the least while the high embodied VR group learned the most.

Covid Campus Simulation

A gamified simulation of a student on a college campus avoiding a COVID-19 infection

Challenge

Teach students how their decisions as members of the ASU campus community can help to prevent the spread of COVID-19.

Project Team

Mina C. Johnson Glenberg, Xavier Apostol, Don Balanzat, Hannah Bartolomea, Anoosh Kapadia, Ricardo Nieland Zavala, Auryan Ratliff, Jude Rayan, Hector Taylor

My Role

Develop Narrative

Work with co-workers and reserach director to create and iterate storyline for the simulation. We also conducted small user studies and communicated with professionals while iterating the storyline to ensure efficacy.

Design UI

Design wireframes, mock-ups, and protoypes to represent the user interface of the actual application. These designs are directly represented in the final simulation.

Attend Weekly Meetings

Meet weekly with research director, co-workers, and professional UX designer to ensure open and transparent communication.

Why an online simulation?

Initial AR Idea

Originally we wanted to visualize how covid could be spread based on social distancing/mask wearing. Thought this would be done best in AR.

Shift Focus

After conducting user studies, the focus shifted to the impact of an individuals on thier probability of infection. Studies showed that this is better understood in 2d.

Campus

We saw a potential for strong influence on in the target audience of students and their impact on campus.

Design Plan

It was difficult to create a narrative that would effectively communicate the importance of a controversial issue—social distancing.

Observations revealed that students could relate to how 8 key decisions on campus change their probability of infection.

Prototypes were designed using Figma. There were many design iterations to satisfy the client. The top two images show some early versions. The bottom images show multiple screens of a later version.

Final Design

The final prototype was designed in Adobe XD, as that was preferred by the client.

User observation showed some flaws in the previous protoype. This final design provides solutions to those issues, as described below.

Changes:

- Key decisions are displayed in a list, rather than a carousel which was determined to be confusing (3).

- Feedback is added to this current design to ensure that users are learning from each decision (4).

- Change an event page redesigned to give users more information about effects of each decision (6).

Project Outcome

Data collected from 96 participants in pre- and post-game surveys reveal an increase in participants’ confidence taking precautions to prevent the spread of COVID-19 after completing the simulation.

This is the first project that I was a lead UI designer on. I gained important experience in creating professional prototypes and working directly with engineers and clients to make valuable iterations.

You can access the web-based simulation at xr.asu.edu/covidcampus

Covid Campus Simulation

A gamified simulation of a student on a college campus avoiding a COVID-19 infection

My Role

Develop Story

Work with co-workers and reserach director to create and iterate storyline for the simulation. We also conducted small user studies and communicated with professionals while iterating the storyline to ensure efficacy.

Design UI

Design wireframes, mock-ups, and protoypes to represent the user interface of the actual application. These designs are directly represented in the final simulation.

Attend Weekly Meetings

Meet weekly with research director, co-workers, and professional UX designer to ensure open and transparent communication.

The Design Process

Initial AR Idea

Want to visualize how covid could be spread based on social distancing/mask wearing. Thought this would be done best in AR.

Shift Focus to Individual Influence

Now focusing on how an person's decisions impact thier probability of infection. Thought better in 2d.

Campus

Thought a good target audience would be students and their impact on campus. Saw potential for a strong influence.

Project Outcome

8 decisions

The final simulation is based on 8 key decisions that an ASU student would have to make on campus, for example whether or not to wear a mask. These decisions directly correlate with the user's probability of infection for COVID-19.

Feedback for Learning

Feedback is given to users based on these decisions. This is meant to give participants information about how or why their decisions have specific consequences and it's goal is to increase learning.

Survey for Data Collection

The final simulation includes an optional survey to analyze user's learning after the intervention.

Platform is not Destiny

Platform is not Destiny: Embodied Learning Effects Comparing 2D PC to 3D Virtual Reality (VR)

My Role

Design pre and post test

Work with research professor and other graduate students to create an unbiased 10 question survey to analyze the significance of platform and embodiment in learning .

Run Studies

Conduct 4 user studies per week by meeting with participants, running them through the experience, and administering pre and post tests.

Analyze Results

Use SPSS to run statistical analysis on collected data, finding a main effect of embodiment and an interaction of embodiment by platform. This showed that active embodiment groups learned more content and that the low embodied VR group learned the least while the high embodied VR group learned the most.

The Research Process

Objectives

Decades of research suggest that increased levels of embodiment can enhance learning. VR lends itself to kinesthetic interactivity that can provide another signal for learning, beyond the usual visual and auditory ones. The objective of this study is to determine how levels of embodiment might affect learning on two different platforms.

Design Methods

The first part of the research process that I was involved in was designing the research methods for the experiment. We used a 2 (2D PC vs 3D VR) x 2 (low vs high embodiment) X 3 (pretest, immediate posttest and one week follow up) repeated measures between subjects design.

Conduct Study and Synthesize Results

The final study included 179 participants and lasted over 6 months. Data analysis was run in SPSS and potential explanations for these results, as well as future directions and guidelines were formed.

Project Outcome

Effect of Learning on Platforms

There was a main effect for embodiment, such that the more active and agentic groups displayed higher post-play content knowledge. Although, there was not a significant main effect for platform, there was a significant interaction revealing that the participants in the low embodied VR group performed significantly worse than those in the high embodied VR group.

Platform is not Destiny

We cannot simply say that “being in VR is always better” for learning, a platform alone can not be responsible for the effect on learning. It also depends on the educational lesson and the applicaiton that is developed for it.

Design Take Away

One design take away for immersive VR learning games is that when agency and control are truncated, there are comparative deleterious effects on learning. The largest learning gains were seen in the active VR environment, so there is evidence that designing active and embodied lessons with meaningful interactivity and manipulation of content may continue to induce significant effects in learning interventions.

VR learning games should also include instances of in-process embodied learning opportunities and assessments, rather than include passive learning objectives or examples. This study found that embedded in-process interactivity can be a significant predictor of reported agency and learning.
